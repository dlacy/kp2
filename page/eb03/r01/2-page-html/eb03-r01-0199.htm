I.    All men are born, and remain, free and equal in rights : ſocial diſtinctions cannot be founded but on common utility.

IV.    Liberty conſiſts in the power of doing every thing except that which is hurtful to another : hence the exercife of the natural rights of every man has no other bounds than thoſc that are neceſſary to enſure to the other members of ſociety the enjoyment of the ſame rights : thoſe bounds can be determined by the law only.
