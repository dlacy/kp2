The female calyx is quadripartite, and there is no corolla ; the nectaria are four; the piſtils four ; and there are four polyſpermous capſules. There are two ſpecies, the roſea and the minor : the firſt grows naturally in the clefts of the rocks and rugged mountains of Wales, Yorkſhire, and Weſt-moreland.
