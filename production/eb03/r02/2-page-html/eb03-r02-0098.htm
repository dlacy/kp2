ROGUE, in law, an idle ſturdy beggar ; who by ancient ſtatutes is for the firſt offence called a rogue of the firſt degree, and puniſhed by whipping, and boring through the griſtle of the right ear with a hot iron ; and for the ſecond offence, is termed a rogue of the ſecond degree, and, if above 18 years of age, ordered to be executed as a felon.
