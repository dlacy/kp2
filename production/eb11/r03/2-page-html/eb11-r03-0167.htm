Picas and Hares.—The remaining rodents, which include two families—the picas (Ochotonidae) and the hares and. rabbits (Leporidae)—constitute a second sub-order, the Duplicidentata, differing from all the foregoing groups in possessing two pairs of incisors in the upper jaw (of which the second is small, and placed directly behind the large first pair), the enamel of which extends round to their postericr surfaces. At birth there are three pairs of incisors, but the outer one is soon lost. The incisive foramina are large and usually confluent; the bony palate is very narrow from before backwards; there is no alisphenoid canal; the fibula is welded to the tibia, and articulates with the calcanéum; and the testes are permanently external. All are terrestrial, and in many cases burrowing, in their, habits, and some of them are of extreme fleetness.
