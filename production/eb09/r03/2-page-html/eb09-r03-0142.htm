The temple of Venus Felix and Roma Æterna on the Velia (see fig. 23) was the largest in Rome ; it was pseudo-dipteral with ten Corinthian columns of Greek marble at the ends, and probably twenty at the sides ; it had an outer colonnade round the peribolus of about 180 columns of polished granite and porphyry. Of these only a few fragments now exist ; for several centuries the whole area of this building was used as a quarry, while the residue of the marble was burnt into lime on the spot in kilns built of broken fragments of the porphyry columns. A considerable part of the two cellæ with their apses, set back to back, still exists ; in each apse was a colossal seated figure of the deity, and along the side walls of the cellæ were rows of porphyry columns and statues in niches.
