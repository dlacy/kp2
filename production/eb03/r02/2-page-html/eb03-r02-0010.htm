RHYME, Rhime, Ryme, or Rime, in poetry, the ſimilar found or cadence and termination of two words which end two verſes, &c. Or rhyme is a ſimilitude of ſound between the laſt ſyllable or ſyllables of a verſe, ſucceeding either immediately or at a diſtance of two or three lines, See Poetry, n0 177, &c.

RHYMER (Thomas the), was a native of the pa-riſh of Earlſtown, in the county of Berwick. His real name and title was Sir Thomas Lermont. He lived at the weſt end of Earlſtown, where part of his houſe is ſtill ſtanding, called Rhymer’s Tower ; and there is a ſtone built in the fore wall of the church with this in-ſcription on it,

Auld Rhymer’s race lies in this place.

He lived in the 13th century, and was contemporary with one of the earls of March, who lived in the ſame place.

RHYTHM, in muſic, the variety in the movement, as to the quickneſs or ſlowneſs, length or ſhortneſs, of the notes. Or it may be defined more generally, the proportion which the parts of the motion have to each other.
