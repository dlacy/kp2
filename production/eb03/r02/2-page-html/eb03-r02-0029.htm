Riots, routs, and unlawful aſſemblies, muſt have three perſons at leaſt to conſtitute them. An unlawful af-ſembly is, when three, or more, do aſſemble themſelves together to do an unlawful act, as to pull down incloſures, to deſtroy a warren or the game therein ; and part without doing it, or making any motion towards it. A rout is where three or more meet to do an unlawful act upon a common quarrel, as forcibly breaking down fences upon a right claimed of common, or of way, and make ſome advances towards it. A riot is where three or more actually do an unlawful act of violence, either with or without a common cauſe or quarrel ; as if they beat a man ; or hunt and kill game in another’s park, chaſe, warren, or liberty ; or do any other unlawful act with force and violence ; or even do a lawful act, as removing a nuiſance, in a violent and tumultuous manner.
