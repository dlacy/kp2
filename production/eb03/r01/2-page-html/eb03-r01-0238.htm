The method of curing rhubarb, as propoſed by Dr Tirruogel of Stockholm, is as follows: " No roots ſhould be taken up till they have been planted ten years : they ſhonld be taken out of the ground either in winter, before the froſt ſets in, or in the beginning of ſpring, and immediately cut into pieces, and carefully barked ; let them be ſpread upon a table ſor three or four days, and be frequently turned, that the juices may thicken or condenſe within the roots. After this procefs, make a hole in each piece, and put a thread through it ; by which let them hang ſeparately, either within doors, or in ſome ſheltereſt ſhady ſhed. Some perſons dry them in a different way : they incloſe the roots in clay, and make a hole in the clay, about the thickneſs of a gooſe-quill, and in this manner bang up each piece to dry ſeparately, that the moiſture may not evaporate, nor the ſtrength of the root be weakened.
