A formation of numerous pseudopodiospores

within Pelomyxa has been repeatedly described, and these have been seen to conjugate equally, the zygote becoming multinuclear. But the possibility of the alleged reproductive cells being parasites has not yet been fully excluded.

Chlamydophrys stercorea is a small Pilose, occurring in the faeces of several mammals, but only forming its characteristic shell outside the body; plastogamic monstrosities are frequent. The nucleus degenerates, and is expelled with some plasm. The chromidia remain inside the shell, and differentiate or aggregate into about eight. nuclei; the cell is then resolved into as many 2-flagellate swarmers, which escape as isogamous exogametes.
